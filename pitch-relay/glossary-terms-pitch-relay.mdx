# Glossary of Terms for Pitch Relay

Pitch Relay introduces several terms and concepts that may be new to users. This glossary provides clear definitions to help you navigate the platform with confidence.  

---

## A

### Approved Pitch

A startup pitch that has been reviewed and approved by the OnePass team for distribution to investors.  

---

## C

### Curated Deal Flow

A list of startup pitches that match an investor’s specific criteria (e.g., industry, stage, location).  

### Credential Verification

The process of validating a startup’s claims (e.g., legal identity, financials) using OnePass’s decentralized identity framework.  

---

## D

### Decentralized Identity (DID)

A self-owned, blockchain-based identifier that allows users to manage their identity without relying on centralized authorities.  

---

## F

### Funding Range

The minimum and maximum investment amounts an investor is willing to consider.  

---

## I

### Investment Criteria

Preferences set by investors to receive pitches that align with their interests (e.g., industry, stage, location).  

---

## M

### Matching Algorithm

The system used by Pitch Relay to match startup pitches with investors based on their criteria and preferences.  

---

## O

### Open Feed

A section of Pitch Relay where all approved pitches are visible, allowing investors to explore opportunities beyond their curated deal flow.  

---

## P

### Pitch

A startup’s submission to Pitch Relay, including details about their mission, product, traction, and funding needs.  

### Pitch Deck

A visual presentation used by startups to showcase their business to investors.  

---

## S

### Selective Disclosure

The ability to share only specific details from a verifiable credential without revealing unnecessary personal data.  

### Startup Profile

A startup’s detailed information, including mission, product, team, and financials, used to create a pitch.  

---

## T

### Traction

Evidence of a startup’s progress, such as revenue growth, user acquisition, or partnerships.  

---

## V

### Verifiable Credential (VC)

A digital credential issued by a trusted entity that can be cryptographically verified by another party.  

### Verifiable Presentation

A tamper-proof presentation of one or more verifiable credentials, shared with a verifier.  

---

## Z

### Zero-Knowledge Proofs

A cryptographic method that allows one party to prove the validity of a claim without revealing the underlying data.
