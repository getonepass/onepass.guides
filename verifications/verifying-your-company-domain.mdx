# How to Verify Your Company's Domains

Verifying your company's domain on OnePass helps establish trust and authenticity. A verified domain confirms that your organization has control over its official website, making it more credible for investors and partners.

## Verification Methods

You can verify your companyâ€™s domain using one of the following methods:

### Email Verification (Recommended)

- A confirmation email is sent to an address using your companyâ€™s domain (e.g., `you@yourcompany.com`).
- **Steps:**
  1. Go to your **OnePass Account Settings** > **Domain Verification**.
  2. Enter your company's domain (e.g., `yourcompany.com`).
  3. Choose **Email Verification**.
  4. Select an email address from the provided list (e.g., `admin@yourcompany.com`).
  5. Click **Send Verification Email**.
  6. Check your inbox and click the verification link.
  7. Your domain will be marked as verified once the link is confirmed.

### DNS Record Verification

- Requires adding a unique TXT record to your domain's DNS settings.
- **Steps:**
  1. Go to your **OnePass Account Settings** > **Domain Verification**.
  2. Enter your domain and choose **DNS Verification**.
  3. Copy the provided TXT record.
  4. Log in to your domain provider (e.g., GoDaddy, Cloudflare, Namecheap).
  5. Navigate to the **DNS settings** and add the TXT record.
  6. Save changes and return to OnePass to click **Verify**.
  7. Verification may take a few minutes to complete.

- Requires inserting a unique meta tag into your websiteâ€™s `<head>` section.
- **Steps:**
  1. Go to your **OnePass Account Settings** > **Domain Verification**.
  2. Enter your domain and choose **Meta Tag Verification**.
  3. Copy the provided meta tag.
  4. Add it inside the `<head>` section of your websiteâ€™s homepage.
  5. Save changes and return to OnePass to click **Verify**.
  6. The system will scan your site to confirm the tag is present.
